Same copy, same CTA — two surface treatments. Pick one per send. Option 1 (On-Card) reads as a quiet inline row that blends into the body. Option 2 (Navy Spotlight) stands apart as its own panel and self-separates, which makes it the stronger choice directly after a primary CTA. In a brief, just call it by number, e.g. “Product Tour — Option 2 (Navy Spotlight).”

Informational Card — grey box

surface #f1f0ee border 1px #e4e2dd heading navy #0a2540 eyebrow #1e88e5

Not a content block — a structural pattern documented in house-style.md, shown here for visual reference beside the navy and soft cards. Wraps explanatory or instructional prose (a report overview, a “how to leverage this” passage) as its own section; the navy heading ties it to the navy-box family. Often used as a pair (overview + how-to) with a 20px gap between cards. Don’t use it for clickable lists (Linked Session List) or ranked points (Numbered Outcome Grid). Example: Q2-2026-GigaOm-Active-Partners.html.
